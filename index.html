About Me

I’m passionate about the intersection of language models and society, exploring both their practical applications and their broader implications. My work focuses on LLMs in digital humanities, with a particular interest in efficient fine-tuning and domain-specific adaptations.

Beyond that, I have a strong interest in computational musicology, combining my background in musicology with data-driven approaches to analyze and interpret music. I enjoy working on projects that bridge the gap between machine learning, text analysis, and cultural heritage.
